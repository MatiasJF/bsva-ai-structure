# Operations — escalation (cross-sub-section)

Operations-wide hard refusals + escalation map. Sub-sections may add to this; they never relax it.

---

## Hard refusals (Mode A) across all Operations

- **Unannounced partner names** in any artifact.
- **Draft or executed contract language** for any specific matter.
- **HR / compensation / performance / equity** data.
- **Board minutes, memos, or resolutions.**
- **M&A candidates, term sheets, due diligence.**
- **Commercial terms paired with a counterparty name.**
- **Private keys / seed phrases / production API keys** (universal).
- **AI-generated imagery of real identifiable people.**
- **Fabricated quotes** attributed to real people.

## Confirm-then-continue (Mode B)

- Pasted inbound content from external parties — confirm redacted before use.
- Event / campaign content referencing a partner — confirm announcement status.
- Content making specific claims about competitors — confirm sources.
- Any draft pitching an unreleased BSVA initiative to an external audience — confirm approval chain.

## Proceed (Mode C)

- Public data research (within source allowlists).
- Own drafts in BSVA voice on public topics.
- Redacted material fully placeholder-ified.
- Internal process writing.

---

## Escalation owners

| Topic | Route to | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Any incident involving Confidential/Restricted leak | DevRel Lead + Security + (Legal if legal risk) | Immediately |
| Biz-Dev content-strategy decisions | Biz-Dev Lead | Before commitments |
| Marketing voice / brand / PR | Marketing Lead | Before publish |
| Legal matter (any BSVA-specific) | Legal Lead — offline process | Always |
| Exec positioning / public statement | Exec + Marketing Lead + Legal | Before publish |
| Partnership name clearance | Biz-Dev Lead | Before any draft that names the partner |
| Event budget commitments | Ops Lead + Biz-Dev Lead + Exec for >$X | Before commit |
| HR / people | HR Lead | Immediately |

## Ops-wide triggers for immediate escalation

1. A draft leaving Operations names an unannounced partner.
2. A draft commits BSVA to a $ amount not previously approved.
3. An external comm is about to be sent without the approval ladder.
4. An Ops artifact has been pasted into an unapproved external tool (any tier below Public).

## Cross-sub-section conflicts

Rare but possible: Marketing wants to publish naming a partner Biz-Dev hasn't cleared. The rule: **Biz-Dev clearance is a blocking dependency for Marketing publish.** Not a nice-to-have — a hard gate.

Similar: Exec wants to reference unreleased financials in a public op-ed. Finance + Legal sign-off are blocking.

## The meta-rule

Operations is where a careless sentence becomes a regulatory filing, a legal action, a lost partner, or a press storm. **When in doubt, escalate.** The cost of asking is minutes; the cost of a misstep is months.
