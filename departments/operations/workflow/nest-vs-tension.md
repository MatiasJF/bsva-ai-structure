# Operations — nest vs tension

Extends `<repo>/workflow/03-nest-vs-tension.md` with Operations-typical cases.

---

## Operations-specific worked cases

| Situation | Nest or tension? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Build a lead list of Tier-1 VCs in blockchain infra | **Nest** under Biz-Dev role | Confidential; public sources only; URL-cited. |
| Research a named partner's public posture | **Nest** under Biz-Dev role | Confidential. If already in private convo, up-classify to Restricted and redact. |
| Draft a partnership proposal shell (placeholders) | **Nest** under Biz-Dev role | Placeholder-only. |
| Request Legal review of an MNDA template | **Tension** to Legal Lead | Restricted session. |
| Request Exec sign-off on >$X sponsorship | **Tension** to Exec Lead | Always a tension, even if informal earlier. |
| Draft campaign copy for an event | **Nest** under Marketing role | Internal → Public. |
| Partner-adjacent marketing (mentioning a partner) | **Tension** to Biz-Dev for name clearance | Blocks publish. |
| Fact-check a blog post about consensus | **Tension** to DevRel / Tech | Cross-dept. |
| Exec statement drafting for an industry event | **Nest** under Exec role (if they're drafting) | Restricted. |
| Board memo | **Never in Claude.** No tension, no nest — out of scope. | Hard line. |
| M&A candidate research | **Never in Claude.** Out of scope. | Hard line. |

---

## Operations clustering

Be especially aggressive about clustering in Operations — cross-dept asks are the primary workload:

- **Marketing → Biz-Dev name clearances**: one tension per campaign listing all names.
- **Biz-Dev → Legal template reviews**: one weekly tension per template category.
- **All → Exec sign-offs**: batched weekly unless urgent.

---

## Never via nest or tension

- **Unannounced partner names** — anywhere.
- **Draft contract language** — anywhere.
- **Specific $ amounts paired with real counterparty names** — redact one or both.
- **Compensation / HR content** — anywhere.
- **Board minutes / M&A / term sheets** — anywhere.

If you catch yourself about to write one of these into a Nestr title or description, stop. It belongs in legal-held documents outside this system.
