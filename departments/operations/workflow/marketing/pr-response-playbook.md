# Marketing — PR response playbook

Reactive communications: responding to inbound press, industry news, or incidents affecting BSVA.

## The rhythm

PR responses are often fast. The cycle still applies; the speed of each step increases.

- CLARIFY in 5 minutes max: situation, ask, audience, stakes.
- CAPTURE immediately — nest under Marketing role (or tension to Marketing if someone else spotted it).
- PLAN-MODE: structured draft + review ladder (Marketing Lead + affected dept lead + Exec if stakes are high).
- EXECUTE: drafts cycle rapidly.
- REPORT: outcome posted with the published response URL.

## What Claude does in a PR response

- Drafts measured, factual responses.
- Runs `external-comm-check` to enforce BSVA voice (no speculation, no hype, no ad hominem).
- Cites primary sources for any factual claims.
- Never improvises quotes.

## What Claude refuses

- **Speculation** about competitors, partners, or market.
- **Personal attacks** against named individuals.
- **Claims outside BSVA's first-party knowledge** (if we don't know, we say so).
- **AI-generated imagery** of real people.
- **Fabricated quotes** attributed to real BSVA people or others.

## The approval ladder

Every PR response, however urgent, goes through:
- Marketing Lead (brand + voice).
- Affected department Lead (factual accuracy).
- Legal Lead (if risk of claim, defamation, or regulatory issue).
- Exec Lead (if reputation / strategy-level).

Fast ≠ un-reviewed. A 15-minute response still has three reviewers; they just work fast.

## Rollback

- If a response lands wrong → open incident tension immediately.
- Correction or retraction per `campaign-cycle.md` rollback.
- Blameless review within 3 business days.

## Triggers to pause, not respond

- The inbound is from a **hostile actor** trying to provoke a quote. → Don't respond until Exec triages.
- The inbound involves **unannounced partner / commercial material**. → Confirm classification with Biz-Dev + Legal.
- The inbound concerns a **live legal matter**. → Legal owns, not Marketing.

"No comment" is a valid, well-considered response. Use it when stakes are high and facts incomplete.
