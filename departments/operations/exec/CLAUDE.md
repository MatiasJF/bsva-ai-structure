# Executive — CLAUDE.md

Extends `../CLAUDE.md` (operations) and the base. Security rules are strict.

---

## Default classification

**Restricted.** Most executive / strategy work is Restricted by default.

Down-classify explicitly if needed:
- Exec-authored blog post or public statement → Internal during drafting, Public on publish.
- Strategy summary for a general audience → Internal or Confidential, depending on detail.

---

## What executives use Claude for

- Structuring strategy documents.
- Drafting general-purpose memos and communications.
- Preparing for speaking engagements and interviews (public content only).
- Summarizing public industry reports and policy developments.
- Draft-level work that will be edited / fact-checked before leaving Exec.

---

## Hard rules

- **No board materials in Claude.** Minutes, memos, slide decks for the board, strategic alternatives under consideration.
- **No M&A material.** Candidates, term sheets, due diligence — out.
- **No compensation / equity / governance details.** Out.
- **No "what if we fired X" / "what if we hired Y" questions** involving identifiable BSVA people.
- **No confidential partner commitments** beyond what's publicly announced.

## What IS fine

- Public industry analysis.
- Published competitor information.
- Public regulatory developments.
- Your own drafts of public-facing statements.
- Structuring thinking on topics that don't reference BSVA specifics.

## The principle for exec Claude use

**Claude is a drafting and structuring assistant for exec work, never a confidant.** Anything that would be problematic on the front page of the business press should not be in a Claude session.

## Escalation

- Exec Lead (CEO or equivalent) — Nestr role: `Exec Lead`.
- Security for any incident.
- Legal for any matter with legal implications.

## Mode recommendation

Exec-tier work in Claude should run in **CLI mode or Desktop**, never web. Sessions should be scoped to a specific folder. Close sessions explicitly (`/clear` or quit) after each task rather than leaving them open.
