# Marketing — CLAUDE.md

Extends `../CLAUDE.md` (operations) and the base.

---

## Default classification

**Internal** for drafts. **Public** for published.

The transition happens on the publish approval, not on draft quality. A brilliant draft is still Internal until it's approved for publication.

Up-classify when:
- The content mentions unannounced partners, products, or timelines.
- The campaign is coordinated with a partner under NDA.
- Pre-embargo press material.

---

## What you'll be asked to help with

- Blog posts, social copy, email campaigns.
- Event materials: landing pages, invitations, follow-ups.
- One-pagers and briefing handouts (use `bsva-onepager` skill).
- Press releases.
- Conference decks.

---

## Voice

**Strictly BSVA voice.** See `guides/for-claude/brand-voice.md` — this applies to every piece of marketing output without exception. No hype, no price talk, no "revolutionary", no "unlock".

---

## Hard rules

- **Never** name a partner in draft marketing copy before the joint announcement. Use `[PARTNER_A]` or remove the reference.
- **Never** paste confidential campaign performance data (with amounts, vendor names) into Claude.
- **Never** use AI-generated imagery purporting to be real (deepfakes, fabricated quotes from named people).
- **Always** verify facts Claude produces before publication. Especially names, quotes, statistics, dates.

## What IS fine

- Public event details, published speaker lists, public sponsorship announcements.
- Your own draft copy.
- Public competitor communications (for reference, never for copying).
- Published BSVA materials.

## Review / approval

Every published piece gets:
- Brand voice review (Marketing Lead or delegate).
- Factual check (by a subject-matter expert from DevRel, Technology, or Education as appropriate).
- For anything partner-adjacent: sign-off from Biz-Dev.
- For anything financial / commercial: Exec sign-off.

## Escalation

- Marketing Lead — Nestr role: `Marketing Lead`.
